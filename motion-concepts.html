Wysdym — Intro Motion Concepts

Motion concepts · Intro video · 5–10 seconds

Six ways to introduce Wysdym in under ten seconds.

Each concept below is a live prototype, not a storyboard — the loops run in real time at the actual pacing. All five use only brand assets: the magenta wordmark, the origami crane, and the June 2026 taglines. They're deliberately different from each other; pick a direction and the timing, copy, and easing are all adjustable.

06

Conventional Wysdym

≈ 15s · from your brief

Conventional wisdom says invest in more GTM agents.

Conventional says give the ones you have a foundation to run on.

The platform every GTM agent runs on.

The wordplay one. "Conventional wisdom says invest in more GTM agents." Then the counter rolls in beneath it — and in line two, wysdym isn't typed, it's the actual logo lettering, in magenta, sitting inside the sentence. The text clears around it, the wordmark glides down to the bottom — staying magenta — the crane folds itself together above it, and the tagline pops in.

  1. 0.0–3.5s Line one: "Conventional wisdom says invest in more GTM agents." — "wisdom" in magenta.
  2. 3.5–7.9s Line two rolls up underneath while line one dims — "wysdym" set in the logo's own lettering, magenta. Holds ~4s for reading.
  3. 8.6–9.5s Everything else clears; the wordmark glides to the bottom, still magenta.
  4. 9.7–11.4s The crane folds in above it, facet by facet — the end frame is the stacked logo lockup.
  5. 11.7–15.0s Tagline pops in; the full lockup holds ~3 seconds so it can actually be read.

Best for: the full 10 seconds — deck opener, website, pre-roll. It's the only concept that argues and brands: the wisdom→wysdym turn is the pitch, and it resolves into the exact stacked logo end frame.

01

The Fold

≈ 8s
Introducing

The platform every GTM agent runs on.

One flat magenta sheet. Crease lines flash across it. It folds — and becomes the crane. The origin story of the brand in one gesture: everything starts from the same sheet.

  1. 0.0–1.0s A single magenta square settles onto black.
  2. 1.0–2.4s Three faint crease lines score the paper — the "plan" before the fold.
  3. 2.4–5.0s The sheet folds away; "Introducing" appears above as the crane facets fold into place one by one.
  4. 5.0–8.0s Crane holds. Wordmark and a large, social-readable tagline rise beneath it.

Best for: the flagship — website hero, deck opener, launch post. It literally performs the positioning: agents are folds; the sheet is the harness.

02

The Unfold

≈ 8s

The platform every GTM agent runs on.

The reverse argument. Start with the crane — the agent everyone's staring at — then unfold it. What's left is the sheet, and the sheet grows until it is the screen. The platform was underneath the whole time.

  1. 0.0–1.6s The crane sits alone in the dark — the thing everyone thinks matters.
  2. 1.6–3.4s Its facets lift away and dissolve, revealing a small flat sheet beneath.
  3. 3.4–5.2s The sheet expands to fill the entire frame in brand magenta.
  4. 5.2–8.0s White wordmark and tagline on the full-bleed magenta field.

Best for: investor contexts — it's the thesis ("don't bet on the agents") as a visual argument, and it ends on the strongest brand frame of the five.

03

Say It Straight

≈ 8s
More agents
won't grow revenue.
Compounding intelligence will.

wysdym.ai

No metaphor, no illustration — just the tagline, cut hard like a film title. The first claim lands, folds away like paper, and the counter-claim answers it. Ends on the wordmark alone.

  1. 0.0–2.6s "More agents / won't grow revenue." slams in on midnight indigo.
  2. 2.6–3.2s Both lines fold backward and drop — a paper-fold exit.
  3. 3.2–5.6s "Compounding intelligence will." builds word by word, "will." in magenta.
  4. 5.6–8.0s Cut to wordmark + wysdym.ai.

Best for: LinkedIn and paid social — it works with sound off, reads at feed size, and the copy does all the work. Cheapest to produce at broadcast quality.

04

The Turntable

seamless loop

The platform every GTM agent runs on.

The rotating one. The crane turns slowly on a lit pedestal like a product reveal — and because it's paper, it nearly vanishes edge-on every half turn, then blooms back. That paper-thin moment is the hook. Loops forever with no cut.

  1. continuous Crane rotates on its axis over a soft magenta light pool, with reflection.
  2. continuous A subtle specular sweep passes across the frame each revolution.
  3. 0.8s in Wordmark and tagline fade up once and hold.

Best for: anywhere a loop lives — website hero background, event booth screen, email signature GIF, virtual-meeting waiting room. Zero-narrative, pure sleekness.

05

Snap to Grid

≈ 9s

The platform every GTM agent runs on.

The abstract one — no origami at all. Blue dots (your agents) drift, disconnected. A magenta grid draws in beneath them. Every dot snaps to the grid, turns magenta, and starts pulsing in unison. Chaos, then a harness.

  1. 0.0–2.7s Eight pale-blue dots wander the dark frame, each on its own path.
  2. 2.4–4.2s Magenta grid lines draw themselves across the frame.
  3. 3.6–4.6s Every dot snaps to an intersection and turns magenta.
  4. 4.6–9.0s Dots pulse in sync; wordmark and tagline appear.

Best for: technical audiences — RevOps, engineers, infrastructure investors. It says "orchestration layer" without a single word, and it's the most category-defining of the five.

Why We're Building the Harness, Not Another Agent

RC Rob Catalano · Co-founder · Jun 25, 2026 · 6 min read

Every vendor will sell you an agent this year. We decided to build the thing every one of those agents needs — and none of them ship.

GTM teams already run three to five agents in parallel. The agents work; the stack doesn't. Each one keeps its own memory, wires up its own copy of the same integrations, and operates with no shared rules and no shared scoreboard. Isolated agents don't compound — and what doesn't compound doesn't grow revenue.

The pattern always repeats

It happened with the operating system, the smartphone, and the CRM: fragmented point solutions, then a unifying layer underneath, then compounding effects that concentrate the value in the layer that sees everything. Agentic AI is at the fragmented stage right now. The layer underneath is unbuilt. That's the harness.

"Don't bet on the agents. Bet on what every agent needs to work."

The harness is five things every agent inherits the moment it connects: shared memory grounded in your business, a library of skills, integrations wired once, governance with receipts, and a feedback loop that turns outcomes into learning. Read, run, write, learn — then do it again, smarter.

how it would ship

The metaphor, load-bearing.

note for Rob ✎

Where it lives: this replaces the static "One sheet. Five folds." card grid on the homepage — same copy, same order, but the visitor physically folds the platform together by scrolling. The envelope of real screens stays right below it.

Why it works: scroll-scrubbed, not autoplay — it runs forward and backward, and a fast flick skips it in a second. Each fold pins its component card around the paper and nothing disappears, so the final frame is the full platform in one view: five cards around the crane, CTA included. That end state is the exact information of the old static grid — the scroll just builds it.

Details to decide: pin length (currently ~1 viewport per fold), whether the final beat auto-plays a moment of the Operator receipts demo, and how much card copy survives on mobile. Reduced-motion users get the finished layout — no pin, no scrub.
